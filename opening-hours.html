Opening Hours &
Ticket Prices

When the War Rooms open, when to actually turn up to dodge the crowds, and what you'll pay in 2026. Short version: book a timed slot, go early, and give yourself two hours.

Opening Hours

The Churchill War Rooms open every day of the year except the three days over Christmas. Entry is by timed ticket, so the "hours" really mean the window your slot can fall in — the last one goes an hour before closing.

DaysEvery day (Mon–Sun)
Hours9:30am – 6:00pm
Last entry5:00pm
Closed24, 25 & 26 December

Hours occasionally shift for late-summer evenings or private events, so the time you pick at checkout is the one that counts. If a slot shows as available when you book, it's running.

Worth knowing

Once you're through the door there's no time limit — the "last entry" only governs when you go in, not when you're kicked out. Turn up at 5pm and you can still take your time.

Ticket Prices

Adult entry with the audio guide starts at £29.75. That single ticket covers the whole site: the underground rooms and the Churchill Museum next door. Children, concessions and family tickets come up as options when you choose your date.

There are also combo tickets that bolt on a guided Westminster walk or a self-guided audio tour of political London — a few pounds more, and handy if you're making a half-day of it. Live prices show on the booking page.

The Quietest Times to Go

This place gets busy, and the rooms are narrow — a coach party ahead of you can turn the Map Room corridor into a shuffle. Two windows are noticeably calmer:

  • The first slot at 9:30am, before the tour groups arrive.
  • After about 3pm, when the morning crowd has cleared out.

Midweek beats the weekend, and term time beats the school holidays by a mile. If you only get a weekend, take the earliest slot you can and you'll still get a good run at it.

How Long You'll Need

Most people spend 90 minutes to two hours. The Cabinet War Rooms themselves take about 45 minutes to an hour at a steady pace with the audio guide; the Churchill Museum is where the time goes if you like a read — some breeze through it, others lose an hour. Budget two hours and you won't feel rushed.

Book ahead

Timed slots sell out on weekends and through the summer. Same-day tickets at the door aren't guaranteed and you'll queue for whatever's left — booking online locks your time and skips that line with a ticket on your phone.
