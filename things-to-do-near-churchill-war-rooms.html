Make a Day of It

What to See
Nearby

The War Rooms sit in the middle of the best square mile of sightseeing in London. You could walk out of the bunker and hit half a dozen landmarks before lunch — here's what's worth your time, and what you can just wander past for free.

Right on the Doorstep (Free)

You don't need a ticket for these — they're part of the walk to and from the entrance.

  • St James's Park — literally across the road. The prettiest of the royal parks, with a lake, pelicans, and a postcard view of Buckingham Palace from the bridge. Two minutes.
  • Horse Guards Parade — the parade ground behind Whitehall where Trooping the Colour happens. Mounted guards on duty most days. Three minutes.
  • Downing Street — you can't walk up it, but the famous gates on Whitehall are a 90-second stroll.
  • The Cenotaph — the national war memorial, standing in the middle of Whitehall. Fitting to see straight after the bunker.

Worth Booking

Three landmarks nearby reward a proper visit. Pairing one with the War Rooms makes an easy half or full day, and each is a short walk or a quick hop across the river.

The London Eye

★ 4.6 · 17,799 reviews  ·  from £25.38

The big wheel is a 10-minute walk across Westminster Bridge, and the view back over Parliament and Whitehall is the best in the city. A 30-minute rotation; go near sunset if you can time it.

Planning tip

Book the War Rooms first — it's the only one here with strict timed entry that sells out. Once you've got your slot, slot the wheel or the Abbey around it. Morning bunker, afternoon Eye works well.

A Simple Half-Day Plan

  1. Start with your 9:30am War Rooms slot — quietest of the day.
  2. Walk out through St James's Park and grab a coffee by the lake.
  3. Loop past Westminster Abbey and Big Ben (five minutes).
  4. Cross Westminster Bridge to the London Eye for the afternoon and the view back.

That's four of London's headline sights in an afternoon, all within a ten-minute radius of one doorway.
